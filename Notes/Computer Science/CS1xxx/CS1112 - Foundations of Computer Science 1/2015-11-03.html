Notes on drawing logic trees

The branches must be directed down the page, and they point to the sub-formulae that the connective acts upon.

A proposition must never have a branch coming out of it.

Brackets never appear in the tree.

Do we need brackets/Precedence?

What does q∧r∨w mean?

We intuitively sort out precedence ourselves when speaking, but we must not be ambiguous here:

  • We apply the connectives in this order, unless brackets tell us otherwise:

    • ¬, ∧, ∨, →, ↔

  • Where there are multiple ∧ or ∨ (or →, ↔) symbols, we combine them on the left first.

In this module we will try to use brackets in most cases to avoid confusion, but there will be a question on the exam paper where we must know this precence in order to do the question.

We must memorise it.
